# Experiment Log

## Objective

The goal of the experiments was to improve robustness of environmental sound classification under clean, noisy, and band-limited conditions.

The focus was not only on model performance, but also on meaningful DSP feature design and reproducibility.

---

## Experiment 1 – Baseline Pipeline Review

**Setup**
- Started from the provided notebook baseline
- Reviewed all editable TODO sections
- Confirmed dataset loading, safety checks, and output naming with `GROUP_ID`

**Observation**
The baseline gave a structured starting point, but there was room to improve preprocessing, augmentation, feature richness, and model robustness.

---

## Experiment 2 – Improved Preprocessing

**Changes**
- added pre-emphasis filtering
- trimmed silence
- standardized all clips to fixed 5-second length
- applied RMS normalization

**Purpose**
To create cleaner and more consistent input signals before feature extraction.

**Observation**
This improved feature consistency across clips and helped prepare the model for varied signal conditions.

---

## Experiment 3 – Robustness-Oriented Augmentation

**Changes**
- additive white noise
- random low-pass filtering
- gain variation
- time stretching
- pitch shifting
- time masking
- combined augmentation strategy

**Purpose**
To simulate realistic distortions similar to the noisy and band-limited evaluation conditions.

**Observation**
Augmentation increased training diversity and made the pipeline more robustness-focused.

---

## Experiment 4 – Rich DSP Feature Expansion

**Changes**
Expanded the feature set beyond basic MFCC by including:
- MFCC, delta, delta-delta
- log-mel statistics with CMVN
- spectral centroid
- spectral bandwidth
- spectral rolloff
- spectral flatness
- zero crossing rate
- spectral contrast
- chroma
- tonnetz
- RMS energy statistics
- temporal autocorrelation and envelope features
- wider-window MFCC representation

**Purpose**
To combine timbral, spectral, tonal, energy, and temporal information into a single feature vector.

**Observation**
A richer feature representation is better aligned with environmental sound diversity and robustness needs.

---

## Experiment 5 – Ensemble Model Development

**Changes**
Replaced a simple baseline classifier with an ensemble voting model using:
- HistGradientBoostingClassifier
- RandomForestClassifier
- ExtraTreesClassifier

**Purpose**
To improve generalization and reduce dependence on a single learning method.

**Observation**
The ensemble approach provided a stronger final modeling strategy for high-dimensional engineered features.

---

## Final Selected Approach

The final submission pipeline uses:
- improved preprocessing
- multiple waveform augmentations
- broad DSP feature engineering
- ensemble-based classification
- automatic generation of model and prediction outputs

This final approach was selected because it provided the most complete robustness-oriented solution while remaining structured and reproducible.

---

## Final Notes

The project demonstrates that good performance in environmental sound classification is strongly influenced by:
- signal preprocessing quality
- appropriate augmentation
- meaningful DSP design
- disciplined experimentation
- clean engineering workflow
